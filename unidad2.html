Comparativos y Superlativos

¿Qué son los comparativos?

Los comparativos se usan para comparar dos cosas, personas o lugares.
Generalmente, se forma así:
Adjetivo corto + -er + than
Ejemplo: My car is faster than yours.

Para adjetivos largos se usa:
more + adjetivo + than
Ejemplo: This book is more interesting than the other one.

¿Qué son los superlativos?

Los superlativos se usan para decir que algo es el más o el menos dentro de un grupo.
Se forma así:
the + adjetivo corto + -est
Ejemplo: She is the tallest girl in the class.

Para adjetivos largos:
the most + adjetivo
Ejemplo: It’s the most beautiful place I’ve ever seen.

Reglas generales

  • Adjetivo corto: big → bigger / the biggest
  • Adjetivo largo: expensive → more expensive / the most expensive
  • Palabras que cambian completamente: good → better / the best

Ejercicios

Completa las frases con la forma correcta (comparativo o superlativo):

  1. This mountain is ______ (high) than the one we climbed last year.
  2. That was the ______ (funny) movie I’ve ever seen.
  3. My phone is ______ (cheap) than yours.
  4. It’s the ______ (bad) day of the week.
  5. She is ______ (smart) than her brother.

Respuestas esperadas:

  • This mountain is higher than the one we climbed last year.
  • That was the funniest movie I’ve ever seen.
  • My phone is cheaper than yours.
  • It’s the worst day of the week.
  • She is smarter than her brother.
